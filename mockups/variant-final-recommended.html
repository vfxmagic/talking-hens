Why your girls have gone quiet

Hi Kerryn,

Every June the same email lands in my inbox: “Jason, my girls have stopped laying — have I done something wrong?” Almost always, the answer is no.

Hens lay on daylight, not warmth. Once we drop below roughly 14 hours of light a day — which we have — their bodies quietly pull back on eggs to put that energy into staying warm. It’s not illness, it’s not your feed, and it’s certainly not you.

  • It’s the shortening days, not the cold, that slows laying.
  • Older and heritage hens feel it more than young hybrids like our Hy-Lines.
  • A good layer feed keeps them in condition so they bounce back come spring.
  • Artificial light can keep them laying — but there’s a catch worth reading first.
