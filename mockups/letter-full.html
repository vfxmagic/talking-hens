Letter from the Farm

The warmest address on the street

Frosty mornings at Merricks — and the coop looks awfully inviting to the locals.

When the weather turns cold, as it is now, your chicken coop quietly becomes the most appealing address on the street. Warm, dry, and with food on tap, it's no wonder rats and mice make a beeline for it.

When we recently asked what was holding people back from keeping backyard chooks, rodents emerged as the number one concern — ahead of foxes, ahead of the neighbours, ahead of the Council. So I've written down everything we've learned and researched about keeping them out, the right way.

A few things that stood out for me:

  • The mesh most people trust is doing almost nothing.
  • A mouse can squeeze through a gap about the width of a pen.
  • The rules on rat bait changed in March — and they affect what you're even allowed to buy.

None of it is hard once you know what actually works, and your flock will be a lot happier for it. We've laid the whole lot out in our latest post, including the one rethink on poison every chook keeper should know about this winter.

Warmly, Jason & Giuliana

Talking Hens, Merricks
